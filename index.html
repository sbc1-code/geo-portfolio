Perspective

My Take on Generative Engine Optimization

The search landscape is splitting in two. Traditional search still matters, but a growing share of high-intent queries now start in AI — ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and Google's own AI Overviews. The question isn't whether to optimize for AI-driven discovery. It's how fast you can get there.

Most companies are still treating GEO as a future problem. I think the competitive window is right now — Q1/Q2 2026 — before best practices harden and everyone catches up.

The advantage goes to teams that understand LLMs from the inside: how they parse content, what they privilege in retrieval, how structured data and entity clarity affect citation probability. That's not something you learn from reading blog posts. You learn it by building with these models.

I've shipped an AI-powered product to production. I use multiple LLMs daily as core workflow tools. I've built NLP search interfaces. That hands-on fluency with how these systems actually work — not just what they output — is what makes the difference between incremental SEO tweaks and a real GEO strategy.

What I'd Focus On

  • Entity authority: Making sure the brand is clearly defined, consistently described, and semantically rich across every surface that LLMs can crawl
  • Structured content at the source: Schema markup, FAQ structures, comparison data, and clear answer-ready formatting that AI models prefer to cite
  • Monitoring AI citations: Tracking how and when the brand appears in AI-generated responses across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and AI Overviews — building a measurement framework for a channel that doesn't have one yet
  • Natural language keyword evolution: Shifting from short-tail terms to the conversational, intent-rich queries people actually use when talking to AI
