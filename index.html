GEO thinking

How I would approach AppFolio's generative search challenge

The search landscape is splitting. Traditional search still matters, but a growing share of high-intent queries now start in AI (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews).

Most companies are still treating GEO as a future problem. I think the competitive window is right now, before best practices harden and everyone catches up.

The advantage goes to teams that understand LLMs from the inside: how they parse content, what they privilege in retrieval, how structured data and entity clarity affect citation probability. You learn that by building with these models, not by reading about them.

What I would focus on for AppFolio

  • Entity authority: Making sure "AppFolio" is clearly defined, consistently described, and semantically rich across every surface that LLMs can crawl. When an AI model builds its answer to "best property management software," AppFolio's entity needs to be unambiguous.
  • Structured content: Schema markup, FAQ structures, comparison data, and answer-ready formatting that AI models prefer to cite. AppFolio's existing comparison blog post is a great start; expanding that pattern across more decision-stage queries would compound.
  • AI citation monitoring: Tracking how and when AppFolio appears in AI-generated responses across ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, and AI Overviews. Building a measurement framework for a channel that does not have one yet.
  • Natural language keyword evolution: Property managers are increasingly asking conversational questions ("what software handles 200+ units with good accounting?") rather than typing keyword strings. The content strategy needs to match.
